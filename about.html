About me

I run operations
and build software.

Based in Dubai. Originally from Zimbabwe. Five years in the UAE holiday home market, and now building the tools that market actually needs.

I started in Dubai's short-term rental market doing the unglamorous stuff: coordinating maintenance, managing housekeeping schedules, handling guests who checked in at midnight and couldn't find the key box. It wasn't glamorous, but it taught me how operations actually work when the pressure is real.

Over five years, the portfolio grew to 350+ units. The team grew. The complexity grew. And so did my frustration with the software we had to use. We were running a multi-million-dirham portfolio on a mix of WhatsApp groups, spreadsheets, and a PMS that hadn't been updated since 2014. Maintenance requests got lost. Guest messages took too long to answer. No one had a real picture of what was happening across the portfolio on any given day.

So I started building. Not because I wanted to become a developer - I didn't know that's where it would go. I just needed a maintenance ticketing tool that worked. That became FixHub. Then I needed something for guest communications. That became GuestCare. Then I decided to go further and build a full property management platform: GGS. Along the way I built PaMarket, an online marketplace for my home country of Zimbabwe, because the same problem exists there - the tools just aren't built for the market.

I'm formalising the technical side now. I completed an Associate of Science in Computer Science in 2026. I hold a CompTIA A+ certification. I'm not trying to pretend I'm a senior engineer - I'm not. What I am is someone who understands property operations at a serious level, who can also build and ship working software, and who knows exactly what problem needs solving because I've lived it.

What makes me different

Most operations people don't write code. Most junior developers don't have five years of running a 350-unit portfolio. I sit at that intersection, and it's a specific kind of useful.

I know the problem, not just the stack

Every tool I've built came from a real operational gap I hit myself. FixHub exists because I watched maintenance requests disappear into WhatsApp. GuestCare exists because I was manually copying messages between platforms at 11pm.

I can speak both languages

I can talk to a property owner about OTA strategy and occupancy rates, and I can talk to a developer about a database schema. That's a narrow set of people. I'm in it.

I've managed real numbers

350+ units, a 40% reduction in maintenance response time, 25% improvement in guest satisfaction, 15% cost reduction. These aren't estimates - they're documented results from five years of operational work.

I ship things

FixHub is deployed and running on a real portfolio. PaMarket is live. GuestCare is in active development. I don't just plan projects - I finish them.
